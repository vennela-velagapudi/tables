Chemical Reaction Timeline
  1. Reaction: Combustion of Hydrogen
    2H2 + O2 → 2H2O
    Explanation: This reaction releases energy as water (H2O) is formed. It is an exothermic reaction, meaning it releases heat.
  2. Reaction: Dissociation of Water
    H2O → H+ + OH-
    Explanation: Water dissociates into hydrogen ions (H+) and hydroxide ions (OH-). This process is essential in acid-base chemistry.
  3. Reaction: Synthesis of Ammonia (Haber Process)
    N2 + H2 → NH3(Incorrect Stoichiometry)
    Corrected: N2 + 3H2 → 2NH3
    Explanation: This reaction combines nitrogen (N2) and hydrogen (H2) to form ammonia (NH3). It requires a high temperature and pressure.
